## Why Visit Palawan?

The air carries a salty breeze, mingling with the scent of lush greenery and tropical flowers as you step onto the shores of Palawan. Known for its stunning landscapes, this Philippine archipelago captivates travelers with its dramatic limestone cliffs, turquoise waters, and diverse marine life. Palawan is not just a destination; it embodies a natural paradise that feels like stepping into a postcard. Beyond its beauty, the island offers a chance to connect with local culture and experience the warmth of its people, making it a unique stop in Southeast Asia.

What sets Palawan apart is its commitment to preserving the environment while providing a rich array of activities for visitors. The UNESCO World Heritage-listed Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park showcases a stunning underground river that winds through a limestone cave system, providing a glimpse into the island’s geological wonders. Palawan is also home to the Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park, a diver’s paradise teeming with lively coral and marine life. Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or cultural experiences, Palawan has something special to offer every traveler.

## Best Time to Visit Palawan

![palawan-philippines](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/palawan-philippines/body_2.jpg)


Palawan enjoys a tropical climate, characterized by two main seasons: the dry season and the rainy season. The dry season, which runs from November to May, is considered the best time to visit, especially from December to March when the weather is pleasantly cool and dry. During these months, you can expect sunny skies and comfortable temperatures, ideal for outdoor activities like island hopping and snorkeling.

The rainy season spans from June to October, with the heaviest rainfall typically occurring in July and August. While the landscape becomes lush and green during this time, heavy rains can disrupt travel plans and lead to higher humidity levels. However, visiting during the shoulder months of May and October can be a great option for budget-conscious travelers, as prices for accommodations and activities tend to drop, and the crowds are thinner.

## Where to Stay in Palawan

![palawan-philippines](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/palawan-philippines/body_3.jpg)


Finding the right neighborhood for your stay in Palawan can enhance your experience significantly. Puerto Princesa, the capital city, offers a range of accommodations catering to all types of travelers. Budget options can be found near the city center, providing easy access to local markets and restaurants. For those seeking more comfort, mid-range hotels are located a bit farther from the hustle and bustle, often featuring amenities like pools and tour assistance.

If you’re looking for a more picturesque setting, consider El Nido, known for its stunning beaches and island-hopping tours. Here, you can find charming guesthouses and boutique hotels that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding cliffs and lagoons. For a luxurious escape, Coron is a fantastic choice, featuring upscale resorts that boast private beach access and spa facilities, perfect for unwinding after a day of adventure.

For a more tranquil experience, the less-visited Port Barton is ideal. This small fishing village offers a selection of budget and mid-range accommodations, allowing travelers to enjoy a laid-back atmosphere while still accessing beautiful beaches and nearby islands.

## Top Things to Do in Palawan

![palawan-philippines](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/palawan-philippines/body_4.jpg)


A trip to Palawan would be incomplete without indulging in the breathtaking beauty of El Nido’s Bacuit Archipelago. This area is famous for its dramatic rock formations and clear water, making it a top destination for island-hopping tours. Spend the day exploring secluded beaches, snorkeling in lively coral gardens, and marveling at hidden lagoons. The iconic Big Lagoon and Small Lagoon are must-see spots that leave a lasting impression with their stunning scenery.

In Puerto Princesa, the Subterranean River provides a unique opportunity to explore an underground ecosystem. A guided boat tour takes you through the cave system, where you can witness impressive stalactites and stalagmites while learning about the area’s ecological significance. This UNESCO World Heritage site is a highlight for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

For those who want to experience local culture, a visit to the Puerto Princesa City Baywalk is a great way to enjoy the sunset while sampling local street food. The baywalk is lined with food stalls offering a variety of snacks that reflect the culinary diversity of the region.

Another noteworthy destination is Coron, known for its incredible diving spots and picturesque landscapes. The Kayangan Lake, often dubbed the cleanest lake in the Philippines, is a popular spot for swimming and photography. Its surrounding cliffs provide a stunning viewpoint that showcases Coron’s natural beauty.

For a more laid-back experience, Port Barton offers a tranquil atmosphere with opportunities for snorkeling and beach lounging. The town is less commercialized, allowing visitors to enjoy a more authentic slice of island life. You can also take a boat trip to nearby islands like German Island for a day of relaxation and exploration.

Nature enthusiasts should not miss the chance to visit the Calauit Safari Park, which is home to various African and endemic Philippine wildlife. This unique safari experience allows visitors to observe animals in a natural setting, providing a different perspective on the island’s biodiversity.

Finally, for an adventure that combines relaxation and exploration, consider a visit to the Honda Bay. Here, you can hop between islands like Starfish Island and Luli Island, where you can snorkel, sunbathe, and enjoy a picnic on the beach. The warm, inviting waters are perfect for a day of leisure and fun in the sun.

## Food and Dining Guide

![palawan-philippines](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/palawan-philippines/body_5.jpg)


Palawan’s culinary scene is a delightful mix of local flavors and fresh ingredients, making it a treat for food lovers. Start your food scene with Kinilaw, a local dish similar to ceviche, made from fresh fish marinated in vinegar and citrus juices. This refreshing appetizer pairs perfectly with the island’s tropical climate and is often available at various eateries and beachside stalls.

Another must-try dish is Lechon, a whole roasted pig that is a staple at celebrations and gatherings. The crispy skin and tender meat make it a favorite among locals and visitors alike. If you’re looking for a quick bite, don’t miss the chance to sample taho, a warm snack made from silken tofu, sweet syrup, and sago pearls, often sold by street vendors.

For a heartier meal, consider trying Lumpiang Shanghai, Filipino spring rolls filled with ground meat and vegetables, which are typically served with a sweet and sour sauce. This dish is a popular choice in many restaurants and is perfect for sharing with friends.

While Palawan has plenty of dining options, street food is where you’ll find some of the most authentic flavors. The Puerto Princesa Night Market is a great spot to sample a variety of local dishes, from grilled seafood to skewered meats. Here, the lively atmosphere and delicious aromas create a memorable dining experience as you mingle with locals.

If you prefer a sit-down meal, many restaurants in El Nido and Coron offer fresh seafood caught daily, allowing you to savor the flavors of the ocean. Pair your meal with a refreshing Buko Juice, served straight from the coconut, to cool off after a day of exploring.
